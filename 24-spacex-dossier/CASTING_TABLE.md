# The 2001 -> 2026 casting table

Mechanism transfer from the validated telecom-2001 system to the AI/space complex of 2026.
A mechanism transfers only if its preconditions exist in the new system; outcomes never transfer.
Working draft — final version goes into the study README with the pilot scores attached.

| Role in the 2001 system | Mechanism that made it matter | 2026 candidate | Casting fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Carriers (WorldCom, Global Crossing, CLECs) — the demand layer that borrowed to build | Debt-financed buildout against speculative demand; fixed obligations meet falling unit prices; the weakest fail first and stop buying | AI labs (OpenAI, Anthropic) burning capital on compute buildouts; neoclouds (CoreWeave tier) | **Partial.** The labs are equity/SPV-financed with hyperscaler sponsors, not coupon debt — failure mode is a financing refusal, not a missed coupon. The *leveraged periphery* (neoclouds) is the truer carrier analog. |
| WorldCom specifically — the serial acquirer whose reported margins peers could not reconcile | Accounting opacity + acquisition treadmill; trust breaks all at once | No confirmed cast. Watch any AI-complex acquirer whose unit economics resist reconciliation | Open casting call — the swarm's 2001 run flagged margin-reconciliation doubt as the tell, before the fraud was public. |
| Lucent — the vendor that financed its own customers | Vendor financing converts customer failure into vendor balance-sheet loss; revenue and credit risk are the same trade | SpaceX (xAI absorbed; compute contracts with termination rights), Oracle-as-credit-channel, hyperscaler equity stakes in their own customers | **Strong fit as a mechanism** — study 27 documented the circular financing independently. SpaceX differs from Lucent in having the Connectivity cash anchor. |
| Nortel — the vendor that bought the top of its own cycle | Acquisition goodwill written down when the cycle turns; guidance collapse as the trigger | The AI names with >100% two-year net-of-market run-ups (study 27's Greenwood-Shleifer-You screen: MU, PLTR, GEV, VRT, AMD...) | Plausible for the screen names; none yet shows the guidance-collapse trigger. |
| Cisco — the FCF-positive survivor that still fell 75% | Even the strongest balance sheet cannot escape a sector multiple compression; it just survives it | TSMC, Microsoft, Meta (already net-of-market negative per study 27), the hyperscalers | **Strong fit.** Survival ≠ price protection — the key investor lesson of 2001. |
| JDSU and the component tier — two layers from demand | Demand reaches components through two layers of inventory; falls later, falls harder; goodwill from stock-funded M&A amplifies | Semis/optics/power suppliers to the AI buildout; the space-component basket (RDW, LUNR tier) | **Strong fit in structure**; timing lag (components peak after the leaders) is the testable prediction. |
| Winstar's bankruptcy — the first credit event that revealed vendor exposure | One mid-size failure reprices an entire financing chain | First neocloud restructuring or major AI-contract termination | The study 27 break trigger, recast. |
