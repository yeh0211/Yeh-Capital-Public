# 04 — License-driven M&A in crypto infrastructure (LIVING TRACKER)

**Status: ongoing — updated as deals print and the regulatory calendar moves.** Last refreshed **2026-06-05**; price data through **2026-06-03**.

**Question.** As US crypto regulation crystallises (GENIUS is law; CLARITY is mid-passage), what is the scarce asset to operate at scale — and do firms buy it or build it? This tracker keeps a running read on the licensed crypto-infra cohort, the disclosed license-driven deal set, and the regulatory calendar that ties them together.

**Current read.** The binding constraint remains **regulatory coverage** — a near-50-state money-transmitter-licence (MTL) footprint, a NY BitLicense, and increasingly a federal trust/OCC charter — and the public deal record shows acquirers repeatedly *buying* that stack rather than building it: **~$6.0bn across 8 disclosed completed deals** (median **$225M**; one further completed deal is undisclosed), plus **$2.4bn** of terminated attempts. Two things the live data adds to the static thesis: (1) the cohort currently sits in deep drawdowns at very high volatility; (2) on the actual GENIUS/CLARITY milestone dates, the cohort has so far **sold the news**, not rallied it. This tracker identifies the pattern and the landscape — **it names no target.**

**What the data actually says.** On the milestone dates so far, the cohort's abnormal return has been **negative**, most clearly at +21d — a "sell-the-news" pattern. Note the first two dates (House-passage 2025-07-17 and GENIUS-signing 2025-07-18) are one calendar day apart, so their +1d/+5d/+21d windows overlap almost entirely and are not independent observations — the effective independent-event count here is closer to **two** than three. This is the honest counterweight to the thesis: even as the regime hardens in crypto's favour, *the legislative milestones themselves have not been cohort catalysts.* The bullish case relies on the discount compressing over a longer arc (or on CLARITY's **final passage**, which has not happened), not on the headline dates.

## 4 — The counter-case still stands

The GENIUS Act's federal stablecoin regime may **reduce** the need for a 50-state MTL stack for stablecoin issuers — if federal charters substitute for state-by-state licensing, the very footprint this note prizes is partly commoditised, and the prize narrows to the scarcer **federal-charter tier**. Which effect dominates is unresolved until CLARITY's final text.

## What it implies — categories, not names

- The most strategically relevant platforms share three traits: a broad state-MTL footprint (40+), a NY BitLicense, and a clean compliance record. Federal trust/charter holders are a distinct, scarcer tier.
- The natural acquirers are payment networks and fintechs that need regulated US rails without a multi-year licensing slog.
- The catalyst that converts pattern into activity is most likely CLARITY's **final** passage — not the interim milestone dates, which the cohort has so far faded.

## Caveats

Not investment advice, and not a prediction of any specific transaction — this identifies a public pattern and the public licence landscape, and deliberately names no targets. The deal-event sample is small (n = 3 listed-acquirer events); the event study is indicative only. Licence counts shift with new approvals. M&A is idiosyncratic — fit, governance and price kill more deals than licensing logic creates. US-centric. The federal-preemption counter-case is unresolved until CLARITY's final text.
