# US equity market state report: the SpaceX listing — as of Friday close, 12 June 2026
All information below reflects what is publicly knowable on 12 June 2026. Nothing later.

## The event
- SpaceX (SPCX, Nasdaq) completed the largest IPO in history yesterday: $75 billion raised at $135 per share, valuing the company at $1.77 trillion. The book was more than four times oversubscribed. An unusually large 30% of the deal ($22.5 billion) was allocated to retail investors.
- First day of trading, Friday 12 June: indicated as high as $174-175 pre-open, opened at $150, closed near $161 (+19% over the IPO price). Intraday the market value crossed $2 trillion. Elon Musk's stake makes him the world's first trillionaire; he holds roughly 84% of voting power through super-voting shares.
- The tradable float is extraordinarily small: about 556 million Class A shares, roughly 4.25% of shares outstanding. At the day-1 close the float is worth about $89 billion.

## The supply calendar (from the prospectus)
- Lock-up releases are staggered: tranches release at roughly days 70, 90, 105, 120 and 135, with the main cleanup at day 180 (December 2026). By day 180, potentially tradable supply reaches about 5.2 billion shares — 9.4 times the IPO float, roughly 40% of the company.
- Index inclusion: Nasdaq-100 fast-entry is expected within weeks; Russell 1000 follows. Index funds tracking these benchmarks are estimated to need roughly a quarter of the current float within six months (about $21 billion of forced buying). S&P 500 inclusion is blocked for now by seasoning and profitability rules.
- The company: roughly $18.7 billion of 2025 revenue (growing fast, led by Starlink connectivity), GAAP losses of about $5 billion driven by AI infrastructure capex. The valuation at the close is roughly 94 times trailing revenue. Independent fair-value estimates published before listing ranged from $780 billion to $1.3 trillion — the stock trades 1.6 to 2.7 times above them.

## The company's situation
- SpaceX dominates global launch (around two-thirds of all mass to orbit) and operates Starlink, the largest satellite constellation. It recently absorbed Musk's xAI and X (Twitter); large AI-compute contracts with two major AI labs are disclosed but carry termination rights.
- The AI labs OpenAI and Anthropic are both reported to be preparing their own public listings; OpenAI filed confidentially four days before SpaceX's debut. Goldman Sachs forecasts a record $160 billion of total US IPO proceeds in 2026.

## The surroundings
- The space-economy complex (Rocket Lab, AST SpaceMobile, Iridium, Intuitive Machines, Redwire, Planet Labs, Virgin Galactic) rallied roughly 40 points ahead of the S&P 500 in the month before the listing; Rocket Lab was added to the Nasdaq-100 the same week. Tesla, Musk's other public company, trades at elevated multiples; some holders treat SpaceX and Tesla as substitutes within a "Musk complex" allocation.
- The broad tape: the NASDAQ is up about 24% over the past year, sitting near record highs with moderate volatility. Money-market funds hold a record $7.9 trillion. S&P 500 buybacks run at a record pace of roughly $1.1 trillion a year. The AI capital cycle is in a heavy-spending phase; financing structures have been shifting from cash to debt and SPVs.
- Retail participation is high: zero-commission brokers, social-media trade ideas, and the largest retail IPO allocation in history. Roughly 810,000 crypto wallets lost about $2 billion on a presidential memecoin launched in January 2025 — the cautionary precedent for retail chasing scarce, story-driven listings is recent and well known.

## Participants in this system
- Retail IPO allocants (hold $22.5B at $135) and aftermarket retail chasers (bought $150-175).
- Index funds: must buy mechanically on Nasdaq-100/Russell inclusion regardless of price.
- Long-only institutions: many anchored to fair-value marks far below the market price.
- Hedge funds: some long momentum, some waiting to short at unlock dates; borrow is scarce with a 4.25% float.
- Insiders and employees: roughly 5.2 billion shares become sellable in tranches through December; many employees have most of their net worth in the stock.
- The space-peer complex and Tesla holders: exposed to rotation if SpaceX becomes the preferred vehicle.
- Financial media and social media: saturation coverage of the trillionaire milestone.
