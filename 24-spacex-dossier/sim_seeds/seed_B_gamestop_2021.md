# US equity market state report: the GameStop situation — as of Friday close, 22 January 2021
All information below reflects what was publicly knowable on 22 January 2021. Nothing later.

## The stock
- GameStop (GME) is a US mall-based video-game retailer: roughly 3,500 stores, about $5B of declining annual revenue, losses in recent quarters. A new console generation (PS5, Xbox Series X) launched in November 2020. Shares outstanding: about 69.75 million.
- The stock closed at $65.01 on Friday 22 January, up 51% on the day on enormous volume. It started the year at $17.25 and traded at $19.95 on 11 January.
- On 11 January, Ryan Cohen — co-founder of Chewy, holding 12.9% of GME through RC Ventures — joined the board with two allies, feeding a turnaround/e-commerce narrative.

## The short position
- GME is the most heavily shorted stock in the US market. Reported short interest is roughly 61.8 million shares against a tradable float of about 50 million — short interest exceeds 100% of the float (data services have reported figures up to 140%).
- Melvin Capital, a roughly $12B hedge fund, is publicly known (via 13F put disclosures) to be positioned against GME and is rumored to be under heavy pressure.
- Citron Research (Andrew Left) said on 19 January the stock would go "back to $20 fast"; his planned livestream was postponed and he reported harassment on 22 January.

## The buyers
- r/wallstreetbets, a retail options-trading forum of about 2 million members and growing by hundreds of thousands per week, has adopted GME as its central trade. A user posting as DeepFuckingValue has documented a long position since 2019, now worth millions, posted daily.
- Retail brokerage activity is at record levels: zero-commission trading (Robinhood and imitators), stimulus checks, lockdown saving, and one-day account opening. Robinhood routes order flow to market makers, principally Citadel Securities.
- The visible mechanics: retail buys large volumes of short-dated out-of-the-money call options; market makers who sell those calls hedge by buying stock; the stock rises; the hedging requirement grows. This "gamma squeeze" loop is being discussed openly on the forum, alongside the explicit goal of forcing shorts to cover.

## The plumbing (knowable but rarely discussed)
- US equity trades clear through the NSCC with two-day settlement. Clearing members (brokers) post collateral that scales with their customers' concentrated single-stock exposure and volatility. A broker whose customers pile into one violently moving stock faces escalating collateral demands.
- GameStop itself has an effective shelf registration; a company whose stock price detaches from fundamentals can in principle sell new shares into the demand.

## The surroundings
- Other heavily-shorted small caps are moving with GME: AMC (cinemas), BlackBerry, Koss, Express, Nokia all saw unusual volume and gains this week.
- The broad market is at record highs (S&P 500 about 3,841 on 22 January). Hedge fund gross and net exposure are near historical highs. Bitcoin ran from $20k to above $40k in the prior three months; SPAC issuance is at record pace. The Federal Reserve is holding rates near zero with open-ended QE.
