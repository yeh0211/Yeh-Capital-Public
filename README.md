# yeh-capital — research notes

Independent quant and markets research by **Hsin Cheng Yeh**. Each note is a self-contained study: a hypothesis I formed, the data and method, the result (including when the result is *no effect*), and honest caveats.

The data pipeline that produces these results is operated privately. This repository publishes **method and findings only** — not infrastructure, credentials, or any third-party confidential work.

> All studies are research / backtested. No live capital, no audited track record. Metrics are reported as method, with confidence intervals and out-of-sample tests, not as a return stream.

## Notes

| # | Study | Question | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | [Volume-sweep microstructure](01-volume-sweep-microstructure/) | Do cross-ticker abnormal-volume sweeps predict next-day semi returns — and does the effect depend on company size? | Clean null at every size. Across a 39-name semi universe split into 4 market-cap buckets (Small <$10B / Mid / Large / Mega >$500B), no sweep type beats its own basket's next-day drift once dependence-adjusted, multiplicity-corrected, benchmarked vs random entry, and split out-of-sample. The size prior (bigger edge in smaller names) shows up only as a point estimate in the smallest bucket's "MIXED" cell (+1.40%) — a five-day volatility artifact: insignificant in excess (p=0.31), exploding with the threshold (+7.77% at Z5.0) and out-of-sample (+2.79%). Size scales the noise, not the signal. |
| 02 | [IPO-anchored VWAP](02-ipo-anchored-vwap/) | Does buying the IPO-anchored VWAP touch actually work, and does it depend on company size? | No edge — and netted against the cohort, slightly worse than random. Across 881 IPOs / 188 liquid names the touch wins ~49% (90d CI [43.0, 54.2]); the eye-catching positive averages are cohort drift, not signal (a random entry in the same names made +144% at one year vs the touch's +25%), and on a dependence-adjusted test the touch underperforms random by 22pts of mean at 90d, significant at one year (z=−2.22, p=0.026). It holds out-of-sample, survives no refinement (first-touch, deep run-up), and no size bucket rescues it — the cohort is structurally tiny (median cap ~$1.1B, none above $100B). |
| 03 | [Due-diligence hub → Tempus tracker](03-due-diligence-news-hub/) | Can mixed-reliability sources become a verified, refreshable single-name read? *(tracked name: Tempus, TEM)* | A **living** single-name tracker: Tempus price / drawdown / relative-strength since IPO + a sourced idea ledger (Signatera-MRD adoption, biopharma data-licensing, path to EBITDA breakeven) grounded in the note record. Refreshable; honest that social coverage is nil and news thin. |
| 04 | [License-driven crypto-infra M&A](04-license-driven-crypto-ma/) | Do the GENIUS/CLARITY milestones move the licensed crypto cohort, and do acquirers buy the licence stack rather than build it? | No, and no-as-stated. The cohort's ~17% beta-adjusted drop after the July-2025 milestones is indistinguishable from a random 21-day window for these names (placebo p ~0.13 vs naive 0.02); cohort beta runs 1.2-4.4 so the old "abnormal vs SPY" was mostly leverage; and the stack is built in-house about as often as it's bought, retiring "buy not build." The deal record ($6.0bn / 8 disclosed completed) is a landscape, not a trade — names no target. |
| 05 | [News-sentiment signal](05-news-sentiment-signal/) | Does news sentiment predict forward returns, or just react to past ones? | Reactive, not predictive. Sentiment tracks the prior 20-day return (Spearman IC +0.20) ~3.2× more strongly than the matched forward 20-day (+0.06); the long-short forward-5-day spread is statistically zero. A trend mirror, not an entry signal. |
| 06 | *Crypto token market structure* | *(linked paper)* How does blockchain infrastructure shape token risk-return and monetary function? | Co-authored, accepted at ICMAIF 2026. |
| 07 | [Intraday decision-time](07-intraday-overnight-decomposition/) | Once the morning move is in, is the rest of the day already decided — and can you trade it? | Conditional. By 11:30 ET the morning move calls the close 79% of the time, but ~69 of those points are pure overlap arithmetic; the leftover gap is a real continuation edge (+5.4 bp gross, CI [+1.1,+10.0], clears zero on 5/5 seeds, stronger out-of-sample, biggest in small caps) across 114 names / 87.6k ticker-days. After realistic costs only the >$500B megacap bucket survives, at +1.8 bp net. |
| 08 | [AI model-launch event study](08-ai-model-launch-event-study/) | Does a frontier-model launch reliably move software (SaaS) stocks? | No — across 11 launches software barely moves vs QQQ (+0.33% over [0,+5]); the famous GPT-4 "crash" was the SVB banking week, not the model; ex-confound the other 10 averaged ~+1%. Only AI-*exposed* lagging AI-*insulated* by ~1 pt/month is real — too small to trade. |
| 09 | [Activist short post-performance](09-activist-short-post-performance/) | After an activist short report, does the stock keep falling — and does it matter who published it or how big the target is? | No tradable drift, in any size bucket. Across 96 campaigns (14 publishers, 2020–26) the 21-day path is indistinguishable from a random day in the same names (edge +1.8pp, dependence-adjusted CI spans 0; the mean even runs the wrong way for a short), the small-minus-large cap difference is flat (−0.4pp), and net of borrow the short edge is negative at every horizon (~20% squeezed >+20%). The only structure is the publisher — and the forensic-shops "win" is one Muddy Waters bounce cohort (pull it out and forensic targets fall −10.7%), so Conditional, not a validated edge. |
| 10 | [Korea memory cycle + leverage](10-korea-memory-cycle-leverage/) | Is Korea's memory-stock retail leverage a death-spiral risk? | Death spiral NOT found. On the available proxy, Korea equities *rebound* after a worst-decile drop (positive at all four horizons; deepest tail 21d +4.8% / 63d +8.3%, significant positive) — the opposite of a margin cascade. Hedged Conditional: the clean Samsung/SK Hynix single-name limb is data-blocked. |
| 11 | [Semiconductor concentration](11-semiconductor-concentration/) | When one stock supplies half an index's gain — and do winners persist? | NVDA is half the semis index (50% of the gain, 46% weight; top-3 = 76%), more concentrated than the Nasdaq — and winners do NOT keep winning year to year (Spearman IC −0.001; prior losers slightly beat prior winners). A risk fact, not a timing signal. |
| 12 | [GOOGL — laggard or not](12-googl-five-year-hold/) | Was Google really a laggard, and what drove the 2025-26 re-rating? | Never the laggard the tape implied. Clean prices show GOOGL +148% (beats 4 of its 5 megacap peers, loses only to NVDA); the "laggard-to-leader" story was a data-corruption artifact, and only the Sep-2025 DOJ antitrust-remedies catalyst (+11.0%, p=0.007) is statistically real. |
| 13 | [Gold vs Bitcoin: collapse insurance?](13-gold-collapse-insurance/) | When equities collapse, does gold protect — and is Bitcoin "digital gold" or a risk asset? | Bitcoin is a risk asset, not digital gold. Across 8 SPY selloffs (≥8%) BTC fell 8/8 and worse than SPY in 7/8 (median −29%) while gold beat SPY in 7/8 (−3%); BTC's crash beta is ~0.94 (1.68 in the tail) and its 90-day SPY correlation roughly quadrupled (0.08→0.37) — converging toward equities, away from gold. |
| 14 | [Alphabet's investment book](14-alphabet-investment-portfolio/) | Is Alphabet actually a skilled investor versus its peers? | Best cost basis, no provable picking skill. Alphabet has the best megacap cost basis on two generational private winners (SpaceX, Anthropic; ~40–55×, +$24bn ≈ 18% of FY25 net income), but its public CapitalG/GV book shows no significant edge over a same-era IPO baseline at any horizon. |
| 15 | [Should you chase IPOs?](15-ipo-chase/) | Do new listings pay off if you buy in the aftermarket? | No — chasing IPOs loses to the market by ~28% a year. 760 US IPOs since 2022-10: a day-1-close entry held one year returned a median −28% vs SPY (CI [−43%, −25%]); only 19% beat the index. Just REITs and profitable software survived. |
| 16 | [Narrow leadership & the index](16-narrow-leadership-and-the-index/) | When mega-caps carry the index and breadth thins, is that bearish — and can you time it? | No warning survives. Across % above 200/50-DMA, the A/D line, new-highs-minus-lows, McClellan and cap-vs-equal momentum, the signs run the wrong way (high breadth weakly precedes lower returns), every conditional 63-day difference is bootstrap-insignificant, and every cash-on-warning overlay trails buy-and-hold on total return out-of-sample (the two that improve Sharpe give up return and fail robustness). The lone residue (% above 200-DMA de-risking) is one-crash evidence. |
| 17 | [Semiconductor supply-chain layers](17-semiconductor-layers/) | Which layer leads — and how much of it is just semiconductor beta? | Completing the roster to 143 names barely moves the leaderboard (Spearman ρ 0.962; only EMS re-ranks, by dilution) — and read market-relative, only 6 of 13 layers beat the semis ETF (SMH); the rest are semiconductor beta. |
| 18 | [Computex event study](18-computex-event-study/) | Do the chip names that headline or supply Computex beat the semis tape after the keynote? | No robust alpha — abnormal vs SMH is statistically zero at every horizon (0 of 40 cells significant) and flips sign across robustness cuts (drop-2021 +1.2%, pre-2021 listings −2.4%); the median name beats SMH less than half the time. Own SMH, not the basket. |
| 19 | [Shorting the semis](19-shorting-the-semis/) | Does conditioning a short on "overbought" semis (RSI>70, >15% above 200-DMA, +20%/21d) beat a blind short? | No — the tradable union short loses at every horizon (21/63/126-day mean −2.1% / −6.7% / −14.8%) and the edge over a blind short rounds to zero; the null replicates out-of-sample and on a second index. The deepest-loss setups are the *worst* shorts (melt-up risk), not a signal. |
| 20 | [Sector rotation](20-sector-rotation/) | As the index rises and falls, does money rotate between sectors — and can you trade it? | Real but untradeable. Capital rotates into cyclicals/tech in bulls and into defensives in bears (defensives beat the market +15–20pts annualized inside the 3 bears; down-capture 0.64–0.94 vs cyclicals ~1.0; pooled cushion +11.5pts; energy the off-axis exception, the only sector ever up in a bear). But a causal 200-DMA rotation nets 0.61 Sharpe vs 0.77 for buy-and-hold, fails to cut drawdown (COVID too fast), and PBO is 94% — a regime/positioning lens, not a timing signal. |
| 22 | [Sector rotation by size](22-sector-rotation-by-size/) | As the AI/tech complex rises and falls, does capital rotate between market-cap tiers — and can you trade it? | No, and no. Across 78 AI/tech names in four cap buckets, size is a symmetric beta dial, not a rotation axis (up-capture ≈ down-capture, spreads on zero, p≥0.11); the "defensive small-cap in bears" reading is a calendar artifact (small caps fell hardest in all four drawdowns, −32% vs Mega −23%); no bucket's bull-vs-bear edge is significant (p=0.27–0.71); and a causal 200-day rotation loses to buy-and-hold (Sharpe 1.13 vs 1.26), cuts no drawdown, and is beaten by its own random-timing placebo (p=0.65). |

## Method notes

- **Validation:** out-of-sample / walk-forward splits, bootstrap confidence intervals, Spearman information coefficient, hit-rate, t-tests with reported p-values; baseline (random-entry) comparisons so market drift is never mistaken for signal.
- **Honesty first:** null and decayed results are published, not hidden — most of the studies here are nulls. A signal only graduates to "interesting" after it survives a larger, out-of-sample test.
- **Reproducibility:** each note states its universe, window, thresholds, and metric so the result can be challenged.
